Mobile Traveler's Guide

Coordinating with a development team in Washington D.C. , I designed the layout for the Uinta Wasatch Cache Forest’s Mobile Traveler’s Guide app. I facilitated meetings with the forest management and members of the public to define the key features available on the application. The app pulls the information directly from the forest website, so knowing what information is the most important was crucial to the final design.

Final Design

Prototyping and Testing

Step 1 | Data Clean up

The Mobile app is linked directly with the current web database and needed adjustments to make the information mobile ready.

Step 2 | Protoype Testing

Using the prototyping software Invision the prototype was sent to all members of the team to test, as well as many members of the public that regularly visit the forest. The feedback was implemented and shaped the final design.

Step 3 | Development Feedback

After the design was sent to the development team in Washington D.C. they analyzed the features and decided what features where possible to implement into the application.
